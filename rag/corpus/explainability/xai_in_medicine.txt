### Overview
Explainable AI (XAI) in medicine aims to make AI model decisions **transparent, interpretable, and clinically trustworthy**.  
In medical imaging, this ensures that radiologists understand *why* a model predicted a certain diagnosis — critical for safety and accountability.

### Core Principles
1. **Transparency:** Provide visual or textual reasoning behind outputs.  
2. **Faithfulness:** Explanations must accurately reflect the model’s true reasoning.  
3. **Human-Centric Design:** Align with clinician workflows and terminology.  
4. **Accountability:** Support audit trails for AI decisions in patient care.

### Key Methods
| Category | Examples | Use |
|-----------|-----------|-----|
| Saliency-based | Grad-CAM, Integrated Gradients | Highlight important pixels |
| Attention-based | Transformer heatmaps | Text-image relevance |
| Concept-based | TCAV (Testing with Concept Activation Vectors) | Explain features like “dural tail” |
| Example-based | Counterfactuals, Similar Cases | Explain via analogy |

### In Clinical MRI
- Enhances trust in automated diagnosis of gliomas, meningiomas, pituitary adenomas.  
- Enables *“explainable second opinion”* for radiologists.  
- Supports education and model debugging (verifying correct region focus).  
- Required by **EU AI Act (2025)** for clinical deployment.

### Best Practices
- Always pair model output with a visualization (Grad-CAM / Attention).  
- Use structured reporting language ("Findings / Impression").  
- Include uncertainty estimates (“Confidence: High / Medium / Low”).  
- Store both heatmap and text rationale in patient record.

### Limitations
- Explanations ≠ correctness; they show focus, not truth.  
- Overinterpretation of visual maps can mislead untrained users.  
- Computational cost increases for high-resolution attention.

### Future Directions
- **Multimodal XAI:** Integrating image + text reasoning layers.  
- **Causal Explainability:** Understanding not just *what* but *why*.  
- **Regulatory Standards:** RSNA-AI interpretability certification for medical software.

### Summary
Explainability in radiology is not optional — it’s a safety requirement.  
When combined with RAG and structured reporting, XAI enables **clinically reliable AI systems** that doctors can interpret, audit, and trust.
