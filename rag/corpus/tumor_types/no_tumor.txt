[TITLE] Normal Brain MRI Appearance and Reference Features
[SOURCE] Radiopaedia MRI Brain Atlas (2023); Naidich et al., Imaging of the Brain, 2022
[TAGS] no_tumor, normal, brain, MRI, T1, T2, FLAIR, DWI, anatomy
[LANG] en

---

A normal brain MRI shows well-preserved anatomy, symmetric hemispheres, and no evidence of mass effect, edema, or abnormal enhancement. 
The ventricles, sulci, and cisterns should appear age-appropriate and symmetric. 
No midline shift or compression of adjacent structures should be seen.

On **T1-weighted images**, gray matter appears slightly darker than white matter. 
Cerebrospinal fluid (CSF) appears dark (hypointense). 
Cortical ribboning and sulcal patterns should be clearly defined. 
Basal ganglia and thalami appear intermediate in signal, and no abnormal bright or dark foci should be seen.

On **T2-weighted images**, the CSF is bright (hyperintense), white matter is relatively darker, and gray matter slightly brighter. 
Normal vessels may show flow voids (black tubular structures) due to fast-flowing blood — this is not pathology. 
There should be no areas of abnormal hyperintensity in white matter or cortex.

On **FLAIR sequences**, CSF signal is suppressed (dark), while brain parenchyma appears intermediate. 
No abnormal bright signal should appear in the periventricular, subcortical, or cortical regions. 
If faint hyperintensity is seen in the deep white matter of elderly patients, it usually represents age-related microvascular changes, not a tumor.

On **DWI and ADC maps**, diffusion should be symmetric bilaterally with no regions of restricted diffusion. 
Restricted diffusion usually indicates pathology such as acute infarction, abscess, or high-cellularity tumor.

After **contrast injection (T1 post-gadolinium)**, there should be no abnormal enhancement in the brain parenchyma or meninges. 
Normal enhancement may be seen in choroid plexus, pituitary gland, venous sinuses, and dural reflections.

**Key normal anatomical landmarks**:
- Ventricles: normal size, no dilation or effacement.
- Corpus callosum: intact and well-formed.
- Basal ganglia: symmetric, no abnormal signal.
- Brainstem and cerebellum: normal morphology and intensity.
- No extra-axial collection or mass lesion.

**Common normal variants**:
- Mild asymmetry of sulci or ventricles (physiological).
- Prominent perivascular (Virchow–Robin) spaces.
- Small arachnoid granulations along the superior sagittal sinus.
- Cavum septi pellucidi or cavum vergae (benign developmental variants).

**Summary interpretation for AI/RAG use:**
If the MRI shows normal signal intensity on all sequences (T1, T2, FLAIR), with symmetric structures, no mass, no edema, and no abnormal enhancement — 
the image should be classified as **no_tumor** or **normal brain**.

**Keywords for retrieval:**
normal brain MRI, no mass, symmetric ventricles, no enhancement, no edema, normal anatomy, no tumor detected.
