### 1️⃣ Overview
Gliomas are primary brain tumors originating from glial cells, accounting for **~30% of all CNS tumors** and **~80% of malignant brain tumors**.
They are classified by the **World Health Organization (WHO)** based on histopathology and molecular genetics:
- Grade I: Pilocytic astrocytoma (usually benign, circumscribed)
- Grade II: Diffuse astrocytoma / Oligodendroglioma (IDH-mutant)
- Grade III: Anaplastic astrocytoma
- Grade IV: Glioblastoma multiforme (GBM)

MRI remains the gold standard for detection, grading, and monitoring due to its ability to visualize tissue contrast, infiltration, and edema.

---

### 2️⃣ MRI Characteristics by Sequence

| Sequence | Description & Diagnostic Value |
|-----------|-------------------------------|
| **T1-weighted (pre-contrast)** | Gliomas appear **hypointense** compared to normal white matter. Helps assess anatomic distortion. |
| **T1-weighted (post-contrast)** | High-grade gliomas enhance strongly due to **blood–brain barrier disruption**. Enhancement is **ring-like** or **heterogeneous**. Non-enhancing cores often represent necrosis. |
| **T2-weighted** | Hyperintense signal due to tumor cellularity and edema. Helps define total tumor extent but may overestimate infiltration. |
| **FLAIR (Fluid-Attenuated Inversion Recovery)** | Suppresses CSF, highlights peritumoral infiltration. Critical for defining tumor margins in radiotherapy and recurrence mapping. |
| **DWI / ADC maps** | High-grade gliomas show **restricted diffusion** (low ADC) in dense cellular regions. ADC inversely correlates with cell density and aggressiveness. |
| **SWI (Susceptibility-Weighted Imaging)** | Detects microhemorrhages and calcifications, differentiating from other lesions. |

---

### 3️⃣ Distinguishing Features vs Other Tumors

| Feature | Glioma | Meningioma | Metastasis |
|----------|--------|------------|-------------|
| **Location** | Intra-axial (within brain parenchyma) | Extra-axial (attached to dura) | Multiple, cortico-subcortical junction |
| **Enhancement pattern** | Irregular, ring-like, heterogeneous | Homogeneous, dural tail sign | Often ring-enhancing with surrounding edema |
| **Edema** | Extensive vasogenic & infiltrative | Moderate | Marked vasogenic, well-demarcated |
| **FLAIR edge** | Ill-defined, infiltrative margins | Sharp margin | Sharp but spherical pattern |
| **Mass effect** | Significant midline shift in large lesions | Moderate | Depends on number and size of lesions |

### 4️⃣ Radiogenomic Insights (Wen et al., 2020)
- **IDH mutation**: Associated with **non-enhancing**, slow-growing gliomas, better prognosis.
- **MGMT promoter methylation**: Indicates **better response to temozolomide chemotherapy**.
- **1p/19q co-deletion**: Suggests oligodendroglioma subtype with prolonged survival.
- Advanced radiomics models can predict molecular subtype from MRI texture features.

---

### 5️⃣ Quantitative Insights (Stall et al., 2010)
- FLAIR-defined target volumes (CTV/PTV) are **~22% larger** than T2-defined ones (p < 0.0001).
- Intersection (T2 ∩ FLAIR) = ~64% of union volume.
- FLAIR-only regions often represent **tumor infiltration** not seen on T2.
- rGTV–FLAIR overlap ≈ **63%**, rGTV–T2 overlap ≈ **59%** — supports FLAIR superiority for recurrence mapping.

---

### 6️⃣ Clinical Applications
- Use **FLAIR + T1 post-contrast** for radiotherapy planning.
- Combine **DWI and ADC** for tumor grading and recurrence differentiation.
- FLAIR hyperintensity beyond contrast enhancement = **tumor infiltration zone**.
- In follow-up imaging, **T2/FLAIR mismatch** can suggest transformation or pseudoprogression.

---

### 7️⃣ Diagnostic Red Flags (for model reasoning)
- Heterogeneous ring enhancement + central necrosis = likely GBM.
- Diffuse FLAIR hyperintensity with no enhancement = low-grade glioma (IDH-mutant).
- Restricted diffusion in core + high perfusion (rCBV ↑) = aggressive glioma.
- Calcified mass with minimal edema = oligodendroglioma.

### 8️⃣ Suggested Radiology Report Template (for RAG synthesis)
Findings:
- Irregular intra-axial mass with hyperintense signal on T2/FLAIR.
- Partial ring enhancement post-contrast with central necrosis.
- Significant perilesional edema and midline shift.

Impression:
- Features consistent with **high-grade glioma (likely glioblastoma multiforme)**.
- Recommend MR spectroscopy and biopsy for molecular characterization.

Next Steps:
- Correlate with IDH and MGMT molecular status.
- Initiate combined radiotherapy and temozolomide regimen.
