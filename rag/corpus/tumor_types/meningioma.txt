### 1️⃣ Overview
Meningiomas are the **most common primary intracranial tumors** in adults, accounting for ~41% of all brain tumors and ~56% of non-malignant ones.  
While **most are WHO grade I** (benign, slow-growing), about **18% are grade II** (atypical) and **1–2% grade III** (anaplastic or malignant).  
Incidence increases sharply with age and is **2.3× more common in females** due to hormonal influences.

---

### 2️⃣ Epidemiology & Risk Factors
- **Incidence**: 9.7 per 100,000 in the U.S. (CBTRUS 2023).  
- **Sex**: Female predominance, especially ages 35–60.  
- **Hormonal factors**: Meningiomas express **progesterone receptors**; growth may accelerate in pregnancy or HRT users.  
- **Radiation exposure**: Most validated environmental risk — linear correlation between childhood cranial irradiation dose and later meningioma formation.  
- **Genetic predispositions**: Germline NF2, SMARCE1, or BAP1 mutations increase risk; MLLT10 variants linked to higher susceptibility (estrogen-mediated).  
- **Other associations**: High BMI, methotrexate therapy, and smoking (in men) modestly increase risk.

---

### 3️⃣ Molecular Pathogenesis
**NF2 gene loss (22q12.2)** is the most frequent alteration (up to 60% of cases) — leading to loss of Merlin and dysregulation of Hippo/YAP and PI3K/Akt/mTOR pathways:contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}.  
Other key recurrent mutations:

| Gene | Location | Pathway / Function | Typical Location | Behavior |
|------|-----------|-------------------|------------------|-----------|
| NF2 | 22q12 | Tumor suppressor (Merlin) | Convexity, parasagittal | Aggressive, high-grade |
| TRAF7 + KLF4 | 16p / 9q | Transcription, TNF signaling | Skull base | Benign (grade I) |
| AKT1 / PIK3CA | 14q / 3q | PI3K/AKT/mTOR | Anterior skull base | Intermediate growth |
| SMO / SUFU | 7q / 10q | Hedgehog signaling | Olfactory groove | Benign |
| BAP1 | 3p21 | DNA repair | Any | Rhabdoid, grade III |
| TERT promoter | 5p15 | Telomere maintenance | Variable | Malignant, poor prognosis |

**TERT promoter mutations** independently define WHO grade III lesions due to their impact on survival (<25% 10-year OS).  
Epigenetic profiling (DNA methylation) now outperforms histology in predicting recurrence and aggressiveness:contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}.

### 4️⃣ MRI Imaging Characteristics
**Typical MRI signs:**
- **Location:** Extra-axial, dural-based lesion with a broad attachment.  
- **T1-weighted:** Iso- to hypointense; vivid homogeneous enhancement post-gadolinium.  
- **T2-weighted:** Iso- to hyperintense; edema variable (more in atypical meningiomas).  
- **FLAIR:** Highlights peritumoral edema and reactive gliosis.  
- **Dural tail sign:** Linear enhancement tapering away from the tumor, seen in ~60% of cases.  
- **Calcification:** Appears hypointense on T2; more common in slow-growing types.  
- **Perfusion MRI:** High relative CBV (rCBV↑) suggests higher grade.  
- **MR Spectroscopy:** Elevated **alanine peak (1.47 ppm)** and **reduced NAA** typical for meningiomas.

---

### 5️⃣ Growth Dynamics & MIB-1 (Ki-67) Index
Study of **342 patients** (Kasuya et al., 2006):  
Independent predictors of high proliferative potential (MIB-1 ≥3%):
- Male sex (OR 2.37, p=0.003)  
- Tumor recurrence (OR 7.57, p<0.001)  
- Non-skull base location  
- Absence of calcification  
Mean doubling time: **<2 years** when MIB-1 ≥3%.  
MIB-1 index correlates strongly with recurrence and WHO grade (Grade I <3%, Grade II 3–7%, Grade III >10%):contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}.

### 6️⃣ Differential Diagnosis
| Feature | Meningioma | Glioma | Metastasis |
|----------|-------------|--------|-------------|
| Location | Extra-axial (dural-based) | Intra-axial | Cortical/subcortical |
| Enhancement | Homogeneous, dural tail | Heterogeneous | Ring-like, multiple |
| Edema | Moderate to severe | Often infiltrative | Prominent vasogenic |
| Diffusion | Restricted peripherally | Core restricted | Variable |
| Spectroscopy | Alanine ↑, NAA ↓ | Cho ↑, NAA ↓ | Lipid/lactate ↑ |

---

### 7️⃣ Clinical Course and Management
- **Surgery**: First-line therapy; Simpson grade I resection (complete + dural margin) minimizes recurrence.  
- **Radiotherapy**: Used for incomplete resections or grade II/III. Stereotactic radiosurgery (SRS) is effective for small residuals.  
- **Systemic therapy**: Still experimental; trials on **VEGF, AKT, mTOR, and somatostatin analogs** ongoing.  
- **Molecular markers** increasingly guide therapy selection. For example, **AKT1/PIK3CA-mutant** cases may benefit from PI3K inhibitors.

---

### 8️⃣ Prognostic Factors
| Factor | Favorable | Unfavorable |
|---------|------------|-------------|
| Grade | I | II–III |
| Ki-67 (MIB-1) | <3% | >7% |
| Calcification | Present | Absent |
| Edema | Minimal | Marked |
| Location | Skull base | Convexity / parasagittal |
| TERT mutation | Negative | Positive |
| NF2 mutation | None | Present |
| Gender | Female | Male |

Ten-year relative survival:
- Grade I: **83–90%**  
- Grade II: **55–65%**  
- Grade III: **14–25%**

---

### 9️⃣ Example Diagnostic Report (for RAG)
Findings:
- Extra-axial dural-based lesion in left sphenoid ridge.
- Isointense on T1, hyperintense on T2/FLAIR, with homogeneous enhancement and dural tail.
- Moderate peritumoral edema without invasion into brain parenchyma.

Impression:
- Likely **WHO grade I meningioma**.  
- Recommend observation or surgical excision if symptomatic.  
- Assess Ki-67 index and NF2 status if resected.
