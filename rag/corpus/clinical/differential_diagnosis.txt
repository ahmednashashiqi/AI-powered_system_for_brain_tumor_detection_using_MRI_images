[TITLE] Short notes on differential diagnosis in brain MRI
[TAGS] differential, glioma, meningioma, pituitary, metastasis, abscess, diagnosis

---

When the image shows a ring-enhancing lesion, consider: high-grade glioma (GBM), metastasis, abscess, or demyelination. Clinical context and DWI/ADC help: abscess often shows restricted diffusion; tumor and metastasis are variable.

When the lesion is extra-axial and dural-based with homogeneous enhancement, meningioma is a leading diagnosis. Consider metastasis or hemangiopericytoma if atypical.

Sellar or suprasellar mass: think pituitary adenoma, craniopharyngioma, or meningioma. Pituitary adenomas often respect the diaphragm and show variable T2 signal.

No mass, normal FLAIR and T2, no enhancement: supports no_tumor or normal variant. Always state "correlate clinically" when confidence is limited.
