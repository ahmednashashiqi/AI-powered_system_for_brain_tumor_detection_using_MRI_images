### 1. Objective
This reference provides standardized structures, terminologies, and templates for AI-assisted reporting of brain MRI scans.  
It ensures reproducibility, diagnostic clarity, and alignment with radiological and neuro-oncological guidelines.  
All generated reports must maintain professional tone, medical accuracy, and structured flow.

---

### 2. Standard Report Structure
Each radiology report should contain these key components:

**(A) Study Information**
- Exam Type: MRI Brain ± Contrast  
- Technique: Multiplanar T1, T2, FLAIR, DWI/ADC, SWI, and post-contrast sequences  
- Contrast agent: Gadolinium-based (dose and safety checked)  
- Clinical indication: e.g., "Evaluate for tumor recurrence", "Visual disturbance", "Headache"

---

**(B) Findings**
This is the most detailed section. Always report normal structures first, then abnormalities.

#### Normal Brain:
- Brain parenchyma shows normal signal intensities on T1, T2, and FLAIR.  
- No mass lesion, midline shift, or abnormal enhancement.  
- Ventricular system: normal configuration, no hydrocephalus.  
- Cerebellum, brainstem, and basal ganglia: unremarkable.  
- No extra-axial collection or restricted diffusion.

#### Lesion Description (if present):
Describe systematically:
1. **Location:** Lobe, hemisphere, or region (frontal, temporal, sellar/suprasellar, posterior fossa).  
2. **Size:** Maximum dimension in mm.  
3. **Morphology:** Well-defined / lobulated / infiltrative / necrotic core.  
4. **Signal Characteristics:**
   - T1: hypo/isointense  
   - T2 / FLAIR: hyperintense (suggest edema or necrosis)  
   - DWI/ADC: restricted diffusion (acute or hypercellular)  
   - SWI: blooming (hemorrhage or calcification)
5. **Contrast enhancement pattern:**
   - Homogeneous → often benign (meningioma, pituitary)
   - Heterogeneous or ring → malignant (glioblastoma, metastasis)
   - None → possible low-grade glioma
6. **Edema and mass effect:** quantify (mild/moderate/severe)  
7. **Involvement:** ventricular compression, cortical invasion, dural tail, optic chiasm, or skull base extension.

---

**(C) Impression (Diagnostic Summary)**
Provide diagnostic interpretation, likely pathology, and confidence score.

Examples:
- *Findings suggest a high-grade glioma with central necrosis and ring enhancement (confidence: high).*
- *Dural-based extra-axial lesion consistent with meningioma.*
- *Sellar mass with homogeneous enhancement and chiasmal compression — likely pituitary macroadenoma.*
- *No abnormal enhancement or mass lesion — normal study.*

If uncertain:
> "Imaging findings are indeterminate. Recommend correlation with clinical and laboratory data."

---

**(D) Recommendations**
- **Glioma:** MRI with contrast every 3–6 months post-treatment; consider perfusion or spectroscopy.  
- **Meningioma:** Surgical or radiosurgical consultation if >3 cm or symptomatic; MRI follow-up in 6–12 months.  
- **Pituitary adenoma:** Hormonal panel (ACTH, prolactin, GH, TSH); ophthalmologic evaluation; MRI follow-up in 6 months.  
- **No tumor:** Routine clinical follow-up only; re-imaging if new symptoms arise.

---

### 3. Reporting Language Guidelines
Use professional radiological language. Avoid uncertain or emotional terms.

| Unclear Term | Recommended Equivalent |
|---------------|------------------------|
| "Maybe a tumor" | "Findings are suggestive of a neoplastic process." |
| "Looks normal" | "No abnormal intracranial lesion or enhancement detected." |
| "Could be infection" | "Differential diagnosis includes inflammatory or infectious etiology." |
| "Nothing serious" | "No acute intracranial abnormality identified." |

**Phrase endings:**  
- “Correlate clinically.”  
- “Recommend multidisciplinary review.”  
- “Further evaluation by MRI contrast study if clinically warranted.”

### 4. Specialized Examples

#### (a) **Glioma**
> **Findings:** Irregular infiltrative lesion in the right frontal lobe, T1 hypointense, T2/FLAIR hyperintense, with heterogeneous enhancement and central necrosis. Mild midline shift noted.  
> **Impression:** Findings consistent with *high-grade glioma (WHO Grade IV, likely glioblastoma)*.  
> **Recommendations:** Surgical biopsy for histological confirmation; consider perfusion MRI for vascularity assessment.

---

#### (b) **Meningioma**
> **Findings:** Dural-based mass at the left parietal convexity, isointense on T1, hyperintense on T2, showing vivid homogeneous enhancement and dural tail. No surrounding edema.  
> **Impression:** Classic appearance of *meningioma*. No invasion of skull or venous sinus.  
> **Recommendations:** Neurosurgical evaluation; follow-up MRI in 6 months.

---

#### (c) **Pituitary Adenoma**
> **Findings:** Enhancing sellar mass (14 × 11 × 10 mm) extending suprasellarly with mild optic chiasm compression. Isointense on T1, hyperintense on T2.  
> **Impression:** *Pituitary macroadenoma* — likely nonfunctioning.  
> **Recommendations:** Endocrine evaluation (ACTH, prolactin, GH), visual field testing, MRI follow-up in 6 months.

---

#### (d) **No Tumor**
> **Findings:** No abnormal mass, signal change, or enhancement. Normal ventricular size and brain morphology.  
> **Impression:** *Normal brain MRI. No tumor detected.*  
> **Recommendations:** No further imaging required unless new symptoms develop.

### 5. Optional Add-ons for AI Report Generation
- Include **probability metrics** (e.g., “Confidence: 0.987, High”).  
- Add **cross-reference to RAG sources** (e.g., “Supporting reference: glioma.txt#0, meningioma.txt#1”).  
- Allow bilingual output (Arabic–English) when reporting for patient summaries.
