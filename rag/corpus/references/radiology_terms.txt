### General Terminology
- Lesion: Any abnormal tissue region (neutral term, not always malignant).
- Mass: Space-occupying lesion, may exert mass effect.
- Signal intensity: Relative brightness on MRI (T1/T2).
- Enhancement: Contrast uptake after gadolinium injection.
- Edema: Increased water content, hyperintense on T2/FLAIR.
- Necrosis: Non-viable tissue, often central hypointensity.
- Calcification: SWI or CT hyperdensity.
- Diffusion restriction: Bright on DWI, dark on ADC.
- Hemorrhage: Blooming on SWI.

### Anatomical Landmarks
- Sellar region → pituitary gland location.  
- Suprasellar cistern → above pituitary, near optic chiasm.  
- Convexity → outer brain surface where meningiomas often attach.  
- Periventricular region → near ventricles (gliomas, demyelination).  
- Corpus callosum → interhemispheric spread (common in glioblastoma).

### Tumor Descriptive Phrases
- “Dural-based enhancing lesion” → meningioma.  
- “Ring-enhancing lesion with central necrosis” → glioblastoma.  
- “Homogeneously enhancing sellar mass” → pituitary adenoma.  
- “No abnormal enhancement or mass effect” → normal MRI.

### Standard Report Vocabulary
- Findings: Objective image description.  
- Impression: Diagnostic summary.  
- Correlate clinically: standard closing phrase.  
- Follow-up MRI recommended: used for longitudinal cases.

### Abbreviations
MRI – Magnetic Resonance Imaging  
FLAIR – Fluid Attenuated Inversion Recovery  
DWI – Diffusion Weighted Imaging  
ADC – Apparent Diffusion Coefficient  
SWI – Susceptibility Weighted Imaging  
CE – Contrast-Enhanced  
WHO – World Health Organization  
ROI – Region of Interest
