[TITLE] Diffusion and ADC Mapping in Brain Tumor Imaging
[SOURCE] EANO Clinical Imaging Protocols (2024)
[TAGS] diffusion, ADC, MRI, glioma, necrosis, ischemia, perfusion
[LANG] en

### Overview
Diffusion MRI measures **microscopic water movement**.  
ADC maps translate this into quantitative diffusion metrics (cellularity indicator).

### Interpretation
| Pattern | Possible Meaning |
|----------|------------------|
| Low ADC + High DWI | High cellularity (e.g., tumor or abscess) |
| High ADC + Low DWI | Necrotic or cystic lesion |
| Mixed ADC zones | Heterogeneous tumor with viable and necrotic parts |

### Clinical Applications
- Differentiate high-grade vs low-grade gliomas.  
- Identify early tumor necrosis post-therapy.  
- Assess treatment response (ADC increase = response).  
- Distinguish abscess (restricted diffusion) from tumor (variable).

### Quantitative Ranges (×10⁻³ mm²/s)
| Tissue | ADC Range |
|---------|------------|
| Normal white matter | 0.8–0.9 |
| Low-grade glioma | 1.2–1.5 |
| High-grade glioma | 0.6–0.9 |
| Necrotic center | 1.8–2.2 |

### Diagnostic Note
ADC maps are critical for interpreting tumor **cellularity** and **aggressiveness**, especially when combined with T2/FLAIR and contrast imaging.
