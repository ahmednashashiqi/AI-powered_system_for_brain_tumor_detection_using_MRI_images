### Overview
T1-weighted MRI sequences provide **excellent anatomical detail** and are the baseline for structural evaluation.  
Fat appears bright (hyperintense), while fluids such as CSF appear dark (hypointense).

### Appearance Summary
| Tissue | T1 Appearance |
|---------|----------------|
| White matter | Bright |
| Gray matter | Intermediate |
| CSF / Edema | Dark |
| Fat / Bone marrow | Very bright |
| Blood (subacute) | Bright |

### Clinical Relevance
- Pre-contrast T1: Used to define normal anatomy.  
- Post-contrast T1 (T1 CE): Highlights regions with **blood-brain barrier disruption**, such as tumors, inflammation, or abscesses.

### Tumor Visualization
- Enhancing ring → Glioblastoma or metastasis.  
- Homogeneous enhancement → Meningioma or pituitary adenoma.  
- No enhancement → Low-grade glioma.

### Diagnostic Notes
T1 is ideal for identifying lesion **enhancement**, **fat content**, and **hemorrhage** stages.  
Often paired with T2 and FLAIR for comprehensive evaluation.
