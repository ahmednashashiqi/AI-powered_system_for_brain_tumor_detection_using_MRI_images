### Overview
T2-weighted images emphasize **water and fluid content**, making them critical for detecting **edema**, **inflammation**, and **necrosis**.  
Fluids appear bright, while fatty structures appear dark.

### Appearance Summary
| Tissue | T2 Appearance |
|---------|----------------|
| CSF / Edema | Bright |
| White matter | Dark |
| Gray matter | Intermediate |
| Fat | Dark |
| Calcification / Bone | Very dark |

### Clinical Relevance
- High signal on T2 → Increased water content (edema, cystic changes).  
- Dark areas → Fibrosis, calcification, or hemorrhage.

### Tumor Visualization
- Most brain tumors appear **hyperintense on T2** due to surrounding edema.  
- Gliomas and metastases exhibit strong T2 brightness with variable margins.

### Diagnostic Notes
T2 complements T1 by revealing the **extent of tumor-associated edema**.  
It’s essential for treatment planning and follow-up imaging.
