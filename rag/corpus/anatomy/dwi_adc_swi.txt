[TITLE] Advanced MRI Sequences: DWI, ADC, and SWI
[SOURCE] RSNA MRI Physics; ACR Neuroimaging Reference (2024)
[TAGS] DWI, ADC, SWI, diffusion, hemorrhage, necrosis, tumor, MRI
[LANG] en

### 1. DWI – Diffusion Weighted Imaging
Measures water molecule motion within tissues.

- **Restricted diffusion (bright)** → Hypercellular tumors, abscess, acute stroke.  
- **Facilitated diffusion (dark)** → Necrosis or cystic areas.  
- Often paired with ADC map for confirmation.

### 2. ADC – Apparent Diffusion Coefficient
Quantifies diffusion in numeric form (mm²/s).  
Used to differentiate **tumor cellularity** or **ischemic lesions**.

| Tissue | ADC Behavior |
|---------|---------------|
| High cellularity (tumor) | Low ADC |
| Necrosis / cyst | High ADC |

### 3. SWI – Susceptibility Weighted Imaging
Detects magnetic field inhomogeneities caused by **blood, calcium, or veins**.

- **Blooming artifacts** = hemorrhage or calcification.  
- Useful for identifying tumor hemorrhage or post-surgical changes.

### Diagnostic Combination
- **DWI + ADC:** Confirms necrotic vs viable tumor.  
- **SWI:** Detects hemorrhage in high-grade glioma or post-biopsy changes.  
- Together, these advanced sequences improve lesion characterization accuracy.
