### Overview
FLAIR (Fluid-Attenuated Inversion Recovery) is a **T2-weighted sequence** that suppresses pure fluid (CSF) signals.  
This allows subtle lesions near ventricles or cortical sulci to be more visible.

### Appearance Summary
| Tissue | FLAIR Appearance |
|---------|------------------|
| CSF | Dark (suppressed) |
| Edema / Tumor | Bright |
| White matter | Darker |
| Gray matter | Intermediate |

### Clinical Relevance
- Highlights periventricular or cortical lesions that might be hidden on T2.  
- Excellent for identifying **tumor-associated edema**, **gliosis**, and **leukoencephalopathy**.

### Tumor Visualization
- Gliomas: bright with poorly defined borders.  
- Meningioma: may show peripheral FLAIR hyperintensity (mild edema).  
- Pituitary adenoma: typically isointense or mildly bright.

### Diagnostic Notes
FLAIR = "T2 minus CSF signal".  
Best used for detecting **pathologic water** (edema, gliosis) rather than pure CSF.
